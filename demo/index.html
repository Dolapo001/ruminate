Reading the herd, not memorising it

The test set is split by animal, so no cow appears in both training and testing — the scores reflect generalisation to new animals, not memorised individuals.

Labels carry a little noise to mimic imperfect vet records, which is why accuracy lands at a realistic ~95% rather than a suspicious 100%. Rare At-Risk events are hardest — exactly where a vet's confirmation feeds back as new training data.
